The Great Dyno Story

Dinosaurs in Dynoville made their candies and gummies with love and every dinosaur who came from lands afar dwelled in the sweetness of their candies.

DYNO loved the gummies as much as he loved sharing them. He wanted everyone to have their wonderful gummies and mallows in the whole wide world so he took off on a journey to share the most delicious of all candies. But DYNO could not have done it by himself, he needed help from his friends who were full of cheer and fun. Tyrannosaurus rex

Tyrannosaurus rex was one of the largest meat-eating dinosaurs that ever lived.Tyrannosaurus was about 40 feet long and about 15 to 20 feet tall.Its strong thighs and long, powerful tail helped it move quickly. Scientists believe this powerful predator could eat up to 230 kilograms of meat in one bite.

 

Brachiosaurus

The Brachiosaurus is a type of dinosaur famous for reaching incredible sizes. The name Brachiosaurus comes from Greek words meaning ‘arm’ and ‘lizard’. The Brachiosaurus had a long neck, a small head and a relatively short tail. They weighed between 30 and 45 metric tons and the length was approximately 26 metres. The Brachiosaurus was a herbivore.

 

 

Corythosaurus

Living near the end of the Dinosaur Age, Corythosaurus, or helmet head, was a large hadrosaur. A hadrosaur is a duck-billed dinosaur. Like other hadrosaurs, it had a toothless beak with rows of sharp teeth located in the back of its mouth. They were plant eaters and grew upto 30 feet long and weighed 5 tons.

 

Stegosaurus

Stegosaurus comes from the Greek words ‘stegos’ meaning roof and ‘sauros’ meaning lizard. They were herbivores. The Stegosaurus was alive in the late Jurassic Period. On average, a fully grown Stegosaurus was around 9 metres (30ft) in length, 4 metres (14ft) in height and up to nearly 5 metric tons in weight.

 

Parasaurolophus

Parasaurolophus lived in the late Cretaceous Period, around 75 million years ago, in what is now North America.Fossils have been found in Alberta, Canada as well as Utah and New Mexico, USA. Parasaurolophus was around 9.5 metres (31 feet) in length and weight around 2.7 tons.

 

Pachycephalosaurus

Pachycephalosaurus was by far the biggest member of the group of thickheaded dinosaurs that are together called pachycephalosaurs. It was a plant eater that walked on its hind legs. Pachycephalosaurus was not as quick as its smaller relatives, but compared to the giant duckbills and horned dinosaurs in its world, it was quite speedy.

 

Spinosaurus

The name Spinosaurus means ‘spine lizard’. The Spinosaurus may have been the largest carnivorous (meat eating) dinosaur ever. Studies estimate that it was around 15 metres (49 feet) in length and weighed anywhere between 7 to 20 tons. The Spinosaurus had a long, thin skull. While the Spinosaurus usually walked on two legs, studies suggest that it may have occasionally crouched on all four.

 

Triceratops

Triceratops was the last and largest of the horned dinosaurs. These dinosaurs are often pictured traveling in large groups, but the biggest gathering of Triceratops ever found consisted of three youngsters. Given its multi-ton weight, it did not walk quickly.
